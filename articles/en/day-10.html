Day 10 | Three-Island Rental Market: 2025 Rents, Yields & How to Choose

Abu Dhabi Real Estate 30 Days · Day 10 · Based on ADREC 2025 public data

Opening · Ready to Copy

Day 7 broke down Al Reem Island (Financial Island) into amenities, price bands, and selection — long-term rental anchor, liquidity first.

Day 8 restored Yas Island (Entertainment Island) as island-wide Master Plan, complete community.

Day 9 clarified Saadiyat Island (Cultural Island) — cultural ultra-luxury, scarce allocation, not more expensive Reem.

After the three-island deep dive closes, Day 10 switches line: If you're renting for now, or already hold and want to calculate rental return — what does the three-island rental market look like in 2025?

In 2025, Abu Dhabi rental moved from "steady" toward "tight." Yas Island (Entertainment Island), Al Reem Island (Financial Island), and Saadiyat Island (Cultural Island) all saw rent growth and vacancy rates outperform emirate-wide averages.

Day 10 lands on three questions —

How much does each island rent for? Where do growth rates differ?

For buy-to-rent, how do gross yields rank?

Who's renting, and how tight are vacancy rates?

Common drivers:

  1. Population: ~7.5% growth in 2024; rental demand continuing to rise
  2. Supply: Few immediately available new units; supply-demand gap especially visible on three islands
  3. Golden Visa: More long-term residents; Saadiyat Island (Cultural Island) and Yas Island (Entertainment Island) attract HNW tenants
  4. Dubai spillover: Dubai rents rising; some tenants shifting to more accessible islands like Al Reem Island (Financial Island)

2025 is a year where bargaining power clearly favors landlords — three-island growth +13% to +16%, all above emirate average +11%.

03 | Buy-to-Rent Yield: How 2BR Apartments Rank

Methodology note: Tables below use post-conflict (2025) rental yield. Under same calculation logic, pre-conflict gross yield is approx. 1.5 percentage points higher; net yield shifts accordingly (Reem gross ~7.6% / Yas ~7.1% / Saadiyat ~6.1% magnitude, reference only).

Net yield must deduct: service charge, management, maintenance reserve, vacancy period, etc.; typically 1.2–1.5 percentage points below gross. Post-conflict rents and bargaining environment are important background for 2025 yields below pre-conflict levels.

Strategy implications:

  • Chase cash flow → Al Reem Island (Financial Island): gross ~6.1% highest among three islands; 2BR market reference AED 1.5–2M, consistent with Day 7 price bands
  • Chase balanced return → Yas Island (Entertainment Island): gross ~5.6%; purchase reference AED 2.3–2.65M (Day 8: AED 1.8–2.8M tier); rent AED 130K–145K
  • Chase asset appreciation + premium tenants → Saadiyat Island (Cultural Island): gross ~4.6%; purchase reference AED 3.5M+ (Day 9 2BR case ~AED 5M tier); allocation logic over spreadsheet

Yas 2BR market reference is AED 2.3M+, not AED 1.35M; Saadiyat at market price often nets in ~3% band. When reading tables, distinguish pre-conflict / post-conflict — current is post-conflict; pre-conflict overall ~1.5 pp higher.
