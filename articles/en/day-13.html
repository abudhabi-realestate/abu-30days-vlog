Day 13 | Villa vs Apartment: Market Structure & Selection Logic

Abu Dhabi Real Estate 30 Days · Day 13 · Based on ADREC 2025 public data

Days 7—9 covered apartments, villas, and price bands on each island.

Day 12's conclusion: allocation buyers default to core off-plan — 71% off-plan, 87% all-cash.

The next blocker: "Apartment or villa?"

In 2025 Abu Dhabi: villas ~28% of transactions but ~42% of value — avg AED 5.8M (villa) vs AED 1.85M (apartment). Two pools, two logics.

62% of villa deals are now 4+ bedrooms (vs 38% three years ago) — long-stay families reshaping demand.

Day 13: ADREC structure + 2025 market data → product-type decision within the three islands.

03 | Apartment logic: Reem default

  • Wide buyer pool — Reem 75% resident expat
  • Deepest secondary — ~26,166 units
  • Entry ~AED 1.3M+; market avg ~AED 1.85M

Order: Reem → Yas → Saadiyat (allocation · 3—5yr)

04 | Villa logic: Yas default at scale

Top factors (2025 survey): school proximity 9.2/10, community safety 9.1/10

06 | Four questions

  1. Budget <3M → apartment · Reem
  2. 3—4.5M + family → townhouse or entry villa
  3. 4.5M+ + 5yr+ → Yas standalone 4BR
  4. HNW + ultra-lux → Saadiyat Lagoons

07 | Close

  1. Villas ~28% count, ~42% value — AED 5.8M vs 1.85M.
  2. 62% villa deals are 4+ BR — plan for schools and safety first.
  3. Apartments default Reem; standalone villas default Yas.
  4. Townhouse = bridge; Saadiyat 4BR ~12M is another tier.
  5. Pick product before island; villa pipeline tight (~1,800 deliveries 2026).
