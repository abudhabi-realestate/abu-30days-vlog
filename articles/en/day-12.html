Day 12 | Abu Dhabi True Cost Checklist: What You Pay at Each Stage

Abu Dhabi Real Estate 30 Days · Day 12 · Based on ADREC 2025 public data

Opening · Ready to Copy

Day 11 walked through the foreign buyer off-plan path in Abu Dhabi — SPA, DARI, Escrow, Snagging, Title Deed.

The next comment question is always:

"I get the process — but what about the money? What do I pay beyond the purchase price?"

Day 12 does one thing: make the numbers clear.

No repeat of Day 11 steps — only three questions:

How much cash at signing, during construction, and at handover?

Where do Abu Dhabi and Dubai differ on one-time fees?

After Day 10 gross yields on three islands, what net return remains after full costs?

Below defaults to freehold off-plan (consistent with Day 11); cash buyers primary, mortgage covered separately at end.

Content Structure

01 | Define the Scope: Total Price ≠ All-In Cost

Many buyers focus only on Purchase Price on the SPA and miss three cost categories:

TypeWhat It IsWhen It Occurs
One-time feesADREC registration, Trustee, agent, mortgage-relatedSigning + handover
Construction paymentsSPA milestone payments into EscrowT0 — before handover
Holding costsService Charge, maintenance, vacancy, managementAnnually after handover

Day 4 covered no holding-period income tax in the UAE — that does not mean zero cost to hold. Net yield deducts Service Charge + operations + vacancy, not tax.

02 | Three-Stage Cash Flow: When Money Goes Out

Example: AED 1.5M off-plan · typical 10/40/50 payment plan · ~3-year delivery (structure varies by project):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Stage A · Signing (T0, ~1—4 weeks)                      │
│  · Reservation / down payment (e.g. 10%)     150,000     │
│  · ADREC registration 2% (often buyer-paid)   30,000     │
│  · Agent fee (if used, ~2%)                   30,000 (opt)│
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────              │
│  Stage A total (no agent)                  ≈ 180,000 AED │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Stage B · Construction (T0—T+36 mo, per milestones)   │
│  · 2nd—Nth Escrow payment (e.g. to 50% cum.) 600,000     │
│  · No rent (opportunity cost separate)         0         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Stage C · Handover (around handover date)               │
│  · Balance (e.g. remaining 50%)            750,000       │
│  · Trustee center fee + VAT (approx.)      4,000—5,500   │
│  · Mortgage: bank fees, valuation, mortgage reg. (if any) separate │
│  · Post-Snagging minor fixes (reserve)     5,000—15,000  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

AED 1.5M unit · no agent · cash full payment · buyer pays 2% registration:

MilestoneCumulative Cash Out (incl. purchase price)
Signing complete≈ AED 180K (10% + 2%)
Mid-construction≈ AED 780K (50% price + 2% cum.)
Handover + Title Deed≈ AED 1.505M (+ Trustee and misc.)

"10% down gets you in" ≠ only AED 150K ready — registration often due at signing; construction milestones require ongoing liquidity. Day 11 bottlenecks #3 (wrong account) and #4 (mortgage pre-approval) both depend on Stage B/C math done first.

03 | One-Time Fee Checklist: Abu Dhabi vs Dubai

ItemAbu Dhabi (off-plan)Dubai (off-plan)Notes
Title registrationPrice × 2% (ADREC)Price × 4% (DLD)Abu Dhabi ~half of Dubai
Off-plan admin feeNone — no Oqood-type fixed admin feeOqood approx. AED 4,000 + VATDay 11 emphasized
Trustee centerAt handover approx. AED 4,000—5,500 (incl. VAT)Similar magnitudeOn-site at transfer / Title Deed
Agent feeCommon 0—2% (negotiable)Common 2% + VATNot mandatory; depends on broker use
NOC (mortgage)When bank / developer requiresApprox. AED 500—5,000+Only with loan
Mortgage registration~0.25% of loan amountSimilarCash buyers can ignore

Abu Dhabi off-plan buyer — commonly unavoidable items:

  1. 2% ADREC — often around signing, not only at handover
  2. Trustee — at handover when receiving Title Deed
  3. First-year Service Charge prepayment — some developers collect at handover (check SPA)

💬 Some developers promote absorbing 2% or DLD/ADREC subsidy — per current contract only; do not assume it by default.

05 | Construction Period: What to Reserve Beyond Escrow

During construction no Service Charge (not yet handed over), but four "hidden" line items:

#ItemTypical RangeNotes
1Opportunity costPersonal cost of capital3 years zero rent — Day 10 gross yield must be read over full cycle
2FX / cross-border transfer0.5%—1.5% / transferOverseas buyers: SWIFT, intermediary banks
3POA notarizationAED 3,000—8,000Remote purchase (Day 11)
4Delayed delivery reservePer contractSPA Anticipated Handover vs actual

Example: AED 1.5M · 3 years · assume 6% gross rent yield

  • If property existed today, 3-year rough rent income ≈ AED 1.5M × 6% × 3 = AED 270K (before holding costs)
  • Off-plan actual rent = 0 → opportunity cost roughly ~AED 270K magnitude (simplified illustration, not promised return)

Off-plan advantage is entry price and timing; cost is capital tied up with no cash flow — Day 12 should be read together with Day 10 post-conflict net yield.

06 | Handover: Balance + Trustee + First-Year Holding

ItemAED 1.5M Unit Example
Balance (50%)750,000
Trustee (~AED 4,200 + VAT)~4,400
First-year Service Charge prepayment (if collected)15,000—25,000 (area × rate)
Snagging minor items5,000—15,000
Tawtheeq registration (if renting immediately)hundreds—thousands

How to estimate Service Charge?

Request AED/ft² / year rate from developer; Reem common 12—22, Yas new stock 18—28, Saadiyat premium 25—40+ (varies significantly by project).

Rough formula:

Annual Service Charge ≈ Net area (ft²) × Rate (AED/ft²)

Example: Reem 2BR · net area ~1,050 ft² · rate AED 16/ft²

→ Annual SC ≈ AED 16,800 (~1.0%—1.2% of price — consistent with Day 10 net yield deduction methodology)

Net yield rough formula:

Net yield ≈ Gross yield − One-time fee amortization (annual) − Annual holding cost rate

Reem AED 1.65M · buyer pays 2% · 5-year hold (illustration):

2% registration pulls 5-year net yield down approx. 0.3—0.4 percentage points — more favorable than Dubai 4%, but not zero.

How to choose (with Days 7—10):

  • Same budget, prioritize cash flow → Reem: lowest signing cash tie-up, highest net yield among three islands
  • Pay more for community and lower vacancy → Yas: slightly higher SC, but Day 10 vacancy 3.8% lowest
  • Allocation play / Golden Visa / long hold → Saadiyat: highest one-time and holding cost base — don't force Reem spreadsheet logic onto Saadiyat

Summary

Day 12 breaks Abu Dhabi off-plan purchase into three-stage cash flow + one-time fees + holding deductions.

Five lines to take away:

  1. All-in cost = purchase price + 2% ADREC (+ agent / Trustee) — not one SPA number.
  2. Signing stage typically needs ~12% ready (10% down + 2% registration), not 10% alone.
  3. Zero rent during construction — opportunity cost belongs in full-cycle return.
  4. Net yield = Day 10 gross − SC / ops / vacancy − registration amortization.
  5. Same 2BR across three islands: Reem lowest signing tie-up, highest net yield; Saadiyat highest cost base — allocation logic first.
