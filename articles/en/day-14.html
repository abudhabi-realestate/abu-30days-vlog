Day 14 | Abu Dhabi Economic Vision 2030: The City Roadmap Buyers Should Read

Abu Dhabi Real Estate 30 Days · Day 14 · Based on ADREC 2025 public data

Day 4 covered three structural edges: sovereign wealth, zero tax, diplomacy — *why* the macro backdrop is relatively stable.

Days 6—9 unpacked Reem / Yas / Saadiyat; Days 12—13 locked off-plan logic and apartment vs villa selection.

The next question: Is there an official city-level plan behind these islands and launches?

Yes — Abu Dhabi Economic Vision 2030, mandated in 2006 and approved by the Executive Council in November 2008: a 22-year roadmap (2008→2030).

Day 14 translates Vision 2030 into what buyers care about: population, industries, infrastructure, and supply on the three islands — with a clear boundary: vision ≠ price guarantee.

01 | What it is — and is not

WhatTop-level economic transformation: less oil dependence, sector expansion, infrastructure, skilled workforce
WhoPublic–private taskforce; benchmarks Norway, Ireland, New Zealand, Singapore
NotA developer pitch; not a promise for any one community; not every project is foreign freehold

vs Day 4: Day 4 = credit & institutions (how deep the reservoir is). Day 14 = where the water flows (industry, people, infrastructure).

02 | One-sentence vision + two priorities

Sustainable, diversified, high value-added economy — globally integrated — better opportunities for all.

Two policy priorities:

  1. Build a sustainable economy — diversification, FDI, competitiveness → jobs & long-stay households (Day 5 / Day 13 context)
  2. Balanced social & regional development — youth, women, regions beyond the capital; still attract skilled expats → foreign buyers focus ADREC investment zones (Day 11)

Seven ongoing policy focuses include business environment, fiscal discipline, financial stability, labour market, infrastructure, human capital, financial markets.

05 | Infrastructure: real estate named in the plan

Power (ADWEA, Vision 2030 text): Large real estate projects on Al-Reem Island, Saadiyat, and Al-Raha Beach are major drivers of rising electricity demand; demand could more than triple by ~2020.

Transport: Airport phased expansion toward ~30M passengers/year; Mina Zayed → Khalifa Port; city metro, emirate-wide rail, links to resort islands discussed.

Tourism (2008 baseline): ~1.34M visitors (2006) → ~2.7M within five years — tied to hotels, resorts, museums.

Urban structure: 2030 urban structure framework plan published; zoning & master planning to protect liveability.

07 | What Vision 2030 does not say

  • Title: National Housing (ADHA) communities — UAE Nationals only — not the same as Yas/Saadiyat freehold product
  • Zones: Foreign buyers = ADREC investment zones / freehold (Day 11)
  • Regional balance: Al Ain & western regions matter in the plan; allocation buyers still default three islands + core zones
